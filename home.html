Dementia

Dementia is a neurological condition characterized by a progressive decline in cognitive function,
affecting a person's ability to perform everyday activities.
It is not a specific disease but a term that encompasses a range of symptoms associated with a loss of memory and other cognitive skills.
Dementia is more commonly seen in older adults, but it can also occur in younger individuals.

♦ What are the signs and symptoms of dementia?
Because dementia is a general term, its symptoms can vary widely from person to person.
People with dementia have problems with:
1.Memory
2.Attention
3.Communication
4.Reasoning, judgment, and problem solving
5.Visual perception beyond typical age-related changes in vision
